About us

The Ghana Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (GIALS), headquartered at No.1 Freetown Link, Okponglo Junction, in Accra, Ghana, is a non-profit organisation that was foundered by the late Professor Stephen Offei, with the aim of enhancing training and capacity building in all aspects of the law in Ghana. Part of its mission is to work with universities, the legal profession, industry, civil society organisations, and the community, to develop programmes of research, consultancy and teaching with focus on social, economic and sustainable development.

GIALS has also been working closely with the Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (IALS) in London. The organisation was inaugurated on June 18, 2015, at a very colourful ceremony that was chaired by Retired Supreme Court Judge, Professor Justice S.K. Date Bah, and attended by many members of the legal community, nationally and internationally. On the preceding evening, Mr Jules Winterton, the then Director of the Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (IALS) in London, delivered a public Lecture on the theme, “Legal Education at the Crossroads”, at the British Council in Accra, to celebrate the inauguration of GIALS.

Courses of Study & Training

Over the years GIALS has offered training by way of seminars and conferences covering a variety of topics. These include:

Virtual Banking Confab 2020
12 August 2020

GIALS continues to offers a variety of courses, seminars (face-to-face and online) and webinars, on an on-going basis, throughout the year, at different locations in Ghana. Upcoming courses and seminars, covering different aspects of the law, are periodically published on the GIALS website and the dailies. At these courses and seminars, participants benefit from interacting with professionals and experts in different areas of law.
