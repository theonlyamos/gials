About us

The Ghana Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (GIALS), headquartered at No.1 Freetown Link, Okponglo Junction, in Accra, Ghana, is a non-profit organisation that was foundered by the late Professor Stephen Offei, with the aim of enhancing training and capacity building in all aspects of the law in Ghana. Part of its mission is to work with universities, the legal profession, industry, civil society organisations, and the community, to develop programmes of research, consultancy and teaching with focus on social, economic and sustainable development.

GIALS has also been working closely with the Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (IALS) in London. The organisation was inaugurated on June 18, 2015, at a very colourful ceremony that was chaired by Retired Supreme Court Judge, Professor Justice S.K. Date Bah, and attended by many members of the legal community, nationally and internationally. On the preceding evening, Mr Jules Winterton, the then Director of the Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (IALS) in London, delivered a public Lecture on the theme, “Legal Education at the Crossroads”, at the British Council in Accra, to celebrate the inauguration of GIALS.

Profiles

Nana Susubribi Krobea Asante, Paramount Chief of Asante Asokore, Ghana, known in private life as Samuel Kwadwo Boaten Asante, is currently the Chairman of the Advisory Council of GIALS. He was educated at Achimota School, obtained his Bachelor of Laws (LL.B Hons) Degree from Nottingham University in 1956, and an LL.M. from the University of London; Kings College and the Institute of Advanced Legal Studies, in 1958. He was admitted as a Solicitor (Hons) of the Supreme Court of England, and to the Ghana Bar in 1960. Nana Professor S.K.B. Asante also read for and obtained a Doctor of Juridical Science (JSD) degree from Yale Law School, USA in 1965. He was a pioneer lecturer in law at the Department/Faculty of Law, University of Ghana, Legon (1961-65) and Acting Head of the Law Department in 1962. He also lectured at the faculty of Law, Leeds University (UK) in 1965-66. Nana Dr S.K.B. Asante has extensive experience in international transactional law and is an international legal consultant, specialising in investment law, corporation law and transfer of technology, law of arbitration and governance. In 1991-92, Nana was the Chairman of the Committee of Experts that drafted proposals for the 1992 Constitution of Ghana. Nana is a Fellow of the World Academy of Arts and Sciences, Hon. Fellow of the Society of Advanced Legal Studies, U.K, Fellow and past president of the Ghana Academy of Arts and Sciences, and Life Member of Clare Hall, Cambridge University.

  • Attorney in the Legal Department of the World Bank, Washington DC, (1966-69)
  • Adjunct Professor of Law at Howard University, Washington D.C (1967-69)
  • Solicitor General and Deputy Attorney-General of Ghana (1969-77)
  • Distinguished Visiting Professor of Law and Business, Temple University Law School, Philadelphia, U.S.A. (1976)
  • Chief Legal Advisor at the UN Centre on Transnational Corporations, (UNCTC), New York, (1977-83)
  • Visiting Fellow of Clare Hall and Visiting Member of the Law Faculty, Cambridge University (1978-79); Director UNCTC (1983-92)
  • Visiting Professor of Law, Faculty of Law, University of Ghana (1991)
  • Director-in-charge, Division of Natural Resources, Energy, Environment Science and Technology at the UN, New York, (1993); and
  • Chairman, Public Utility Regulatory Commission of Ghana (1997-2002)

Professor Dr. Edmund Kwaw is an Associate Professor of Law, and Head of Department at the Faculty of Law of Wisconsin International University College, and the Acting Executive Director of the GIALS. He was educated at Achimota School, and holds a Bachelor of Arts, with Honours (B.A. Hons) degree from the University of Ghana Legon, a Bachelor of Laws with Honours (LL.B. Hons) degree from the University of Leeds, and a Master of Laws (LL.M) degree from Queens University, Kingston, Ontario, Canada. Professor Kwaw also obtained his doctorate degree in law, the Doctor of Jurisprudence (D.Jur) degree, from Osgoode Hall Law School of York University, Toronto, Ontario, Canada.

Professor Kwaw has held numerous teaching and administrative positions at tertiary institutions, nationally and internationally, including the UPSA Law School, of the University of Professional Studies, Accra, where he was Head of the Department of Private Law; the Faculty of Law, Governance and International Relations at Kings University College, where he was Dean as well as Vice-President of the University; the Faculty of Law of the University of Cape Coast, the Faculty of Law of Queens University, Ontario, Canada; Osgoode Hall Law School of York University, Toronto; the Faculty of Law of the University of Windsor, and the Seneca College of Applied Arts and Technology, in Toronto.

He is an external examiner for law programmes at several universities; nationally and internationally, including the Bachelor of Laws (LLB) Programme of Mountcrest University College, Ghana, and the Doctoral Degree (LL.B/PhD) Programmes at the Universities of Johannesburg and Pretoria in South Africa. He has provided consultancy services to governmental bodies, including training Members of the Parliament of Ghana on International Loan Agreements and Financing Arrangements. He is the author of several academic textbooks, and numerous peer-reviewed journal papers in national and international journals. Professor Kwaw is also an internationally acclaimed author of fiction, and a third degree black belt in Shotokan Karate.

Mr Jules Winterton is the Adviser on Strategic Development of GIALS. Currently, he is a Senior Associate Research Fellow at the Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (IALS)/School of Advanced Study, University of London. Jules Winterton was Director and Librarian of the Institute of Advanced Legal Studies (IALS)/School of Advanced Study, University of London from 2013-18. He became Librarian of the IALS in 1991 and was Acting Director from 2003-04 and 2012-13. Before working at the IALS, he worked at the Institute of Classical Studies and at Queen Mary College, University of London.

He was President of the British and Irish Association of Law Librarians (BIALL) in 1994/95 and President of the International Association of Law Libraries (IALL) from 2004-2010, responsible for international conferences in Florence, Mumbai, St. Petersburg, Puerto Rico, Istanbul and The Hague. He received the Wildy BIALL Law Librarian of the Year Award in 2010. He is an honorary Life Member of both associations. In addition, he has been a visiting research fellow at the Max Planck Institute for Comparative and International Private Law in Hamburg and at the University of Florence. He was a Trustee of the British and Irish Legal Information Institute, which is based at the IALS, from its start in the UK in 2000 until 2018, a Director of the LLMC Digital consortium in the US on two occasions, most recently 2015-2018, and is a member of the Board of the Ghana Institute of Advanced Legal Studies. He is Convenor of the Libraries Committee of the Society of Legal Scholars which is responsible for A Statement of Standards for University Law Library Provision in the United Kingdom.
